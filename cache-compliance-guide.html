The Golden Rules
Before writing a single word of marketing content, understand these three non-negotiable principles. Everything else in this guide flows from them.
🚫
Never Do
  • Promise or imply returns
  • Give personal financial advice
  • Use prohibited terms
  • Omit risks when mentioning benefits
  • Use unverified statistics
  • Misrepresent ESG credentials
  • Allow unlicensed 3rd parties to give advice
⚠️
Requires Care
  • General advice (needs documented rationale)
  • Past performance data
  • Projections & forecasts
  • Comparisons with other products
  • Testimonials from customers
  • ESG / sustainability claims
  • Fee comparisons
✅
Generally Safe
  • Verified factual statements (with source)
  • Describing how the product works
  • Explaining the investment process
  • Factual product features
  • Directing people to the PDS/TMD
  • Providing compliant disclaimers
⚡
The overarching rule (Corporations Act s1041H) You must not engage in conduct that is misleading or deceptive — or likely to mislead or deceive — in relation to a financial product. This applies to both wholesale and retail investors. There is no minimum threshold. Even a small inaccuracy counts. Intent is irrelevant — you can breach the law accidentally.
📋
All promotional material must be submitted to Cache before use. Submit via the Cache Marketing Jira Board (contact sasha.inquimbert@cacheinvest.com.au for access). Attach as a Word document for mark-up. Do not use Google Docs links or PDFs that can't be marked up.
⚡ Must
You must be licensed
Anyone providing financial product advice must be an authorised representative of Cache's AFSL. Both the corporate entity AND the individual must be registered with ASIC.
⚡ Must
RG146 training required
Anyone speaking to retail clients, producing promotional materials, or involved in distribution must be RG146 compliant. Cache requires 20 hours of CPD per year. Submit your training register to Cache annually.
⚡ Must
TMD must exist before marketing
A valid Target Market Determination (TMD) must be in place before you can promote a retail fund to retail clients. All marketing must align with — not contradict — the TMD. This is a civil penalty provision under s994D.
⚡ Must
ASIC considers the total impression
ASIC assesses whether content is misleading based on the overall impression — including pictures, animations, tone, and language. A technically accurate statement can still be misleading in context.
Content types
Advice vs Factual Information
Every piece of content you create falls into one of three categories. Knowing which category you're in determines what rules apply to you.
✅
Preferred approach — lowest regulatory risk. Factual information is objectively verifiable. The truth or accuracy cannot reasonably be questioned. Always include a source.
✅ Factual Information — Examples
✅ Safe to say
"The ASX200 rose 17% in 2021."
Verifiable fact. Include source (e.g. ASX website).
✅ Safe to say
"Our Fund invests primarily in Australian and international ETFs."
Factual product description. Cross-check against PDS.
⚡ Must
Always include a source
Every factual claim needs a source — hyperlink in the body or under a "Sources" section. We will ask for it if missing.
✅ Tip
Soften superlatives
"We are the fastest growing company" → hard to verify.
"We are a fast-growing company" → much safer, still needs support.
⚠️
Use with care. Requires documented rationale. General advice involves a qualitative judgement or recommendation about a financial product. Cache may ask you to remove advice elements from your content.
⚠️ General Advice — Examples
❌ General advice
"Now may be a good time to invest in shares."
This is a qualitative judgement. If you want to say this, you need a documented briefing note explaining why now is better than other times.
❌ General advice
"Invest now in one of Australia's leading ethical funds!"
This is a recommendation. It requires documented reasoning and a general advice disclaimer.
⚡ Must
Document your reasoning
If you make a qualitative statement, you must genuinely hold that opinion AND be able to provide comprehensive, documented reasons for it — to a standard a professional financial services provider could reasonably rely on.
🚫
Never provide personal financial advice. As an Authorised Representative of Cache's AFSL, you are NOT authorised to provide personal financial advice. Only registered financial planners can do this.
🚫 Personal Advice — Always Prohibited
🚫 Never say
"Based on your salary and current investments, you should invest $20,000 per year in this ETF."
🚫 Never say
"Our product would be great for you." / "You will love our product." / "Our product was designed for people just like you!"
High risk area
Future Performance & Returns
This is one of the most commonly breached areas. Any statement that implies a positive future return is almost always prohibited — even vague ones.
🚫
Cache will NOT approve vague or non-specific future return statements. Representations about future matters must be based on reasonable grounds. If they aren't, the law treats them as misleading — automatically.
Future performance — what's allowed vs what's not
🚫 Prohibited
"Get rich with us."

"Earn up to 12% p.a."

"Start seeing your money grow."

"Make money on your money."

"Save with us."

"Earn high returns with low risk."

"To the moon baby."
✅ Acceptable
"You can start investing now via our App."

"You can begin your investing journey with us."

"This product is suitable for someone wanting high returns and willing to accept high risk." (only if consistent with the TMD)
Focus on the process, not implied outcomes.
Projections & Forecasts — Allowed Only In These Circumstances
⚡ Condition 1
Index-tracking products only
Projections are only permitted for products tracking a specific index (e.g. an ETF tracking the S&P/ASX 200). Not for actively managed funds.
⚡ Condition 2
Minimum 10 years of data
You must have sufficient historical data on the index (at least 10 years) to support the return and volatility assumptions in the forecast.
⚡ Condition 3
Full methodology provided
You must provide a sufficiently detailed explanation of how the forecast was generated — detailed enough that Cache can precisely replicate it.
⚡ Condition 4
Update every 6 months
All forecasts must be reviewed and updated every 6 months to ensure they remain current and not misleading.
⚡ Must include in all forecasts
Mandatory statements alongside any projection
1. The specific methodology and data used to generate the forecast.
2. All assumptions underpinning the forecast.
3. A statement that forecasted returns may not arise and the client's balance may fall.
4. A reference to the Product Disclosure Statement.
Requires care
Past Performance & Returns
Past performance can be used, but it comes with strict rules on calculation, presentation, and mandatory disclaimers.
⚡ Must
Calculate net of fees and taxes
You cannot show a raw change in unit price if there are fees charged separately. Returns must be net of investment fees and taxes. Where some fees can't be deducted, clearly state this.
⚡ Must
Display the mandatory disclaimer
Directly under or next to any performance data you must include: "These returns are not a projection. Actual returns may differ and can be positive or negative. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns."
🚫 Don't
Cherry-pick short periods
Never show only a good short-term period without also showing the broader timeframe. Any performance under 12 months must NOT be annualised and must clearly state the exact period it covers.
🚫 Don't
Omit the benchmark
If your fund has a benchmark, you must show it alongside performance. Advertising 5% returns when the benchmark returned 15% in the same period is misleading.
🚫 Don't
Aggregate multiple investment options
If your fund has multiple investment options, show performance for each option separately. Never blend them together.
🚫 Don't
Use hypothetical stats that imply higher returns
If the ASX returned 10% and your fund returned 8%, you cannot use the 10% figure to imply what investors might expect. You can only use external stats if your fund performed better.
Past performance — rewrite example
🚫 Wrong
"The Fund has provided returns of over 20% to its investors over the last 5 years."
No elaboration, no disclaimer, no specification of which option or period.
✅ Correct
"The Balanced Investment Option returned 13.4% for the year to 30 June 2021.*

*Returns are net of investment fees and taxes. These returns are not a projection. Actual returns may differ, and can be positive or negative. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns."
Balance required
Risks & Benefits
Every time you mention a benefit of your product — even indirectly — you must also mention the associated risk. This is the balanced message requirement.
⚠️
The rule: Whenever your content implies or states a benefit (ease of investing, low fees, compound interest, diversification), you must include a corresponding risk statement. This applies even to educational content that isn't specifically about your product.
Risk balance — example rewrite
🚫 Wrong — benefit with no risk
"Our App provides an easy, all-in-one destination where you can invest in some of Australia's most popular ETFs. Signing-up is easy — start your investment journey today!"
No risk mention = automatic problem.
✅ Correct — benefit balanced with risk
"Our App provides an easy, all-in-one destination where you can invest in some of Australia's most popular ETFs. Signing-up is easy — start your investment journey today!**

**There are risks to investing in ETFs. You may lose money invested. Please read the Product Disclosure Statement, available here."
⚡ Minimum risk disclaimer
For short-form / social media content
"All investments carry risk. You may lose your money invested. For more information please read the Product Disclosure Statement."

Longer advertisements that detail specific benefits may require a more detailed risk description.
Common mistake area
Fees & Costs
Fee disclosures must give a realistic picture. Vague or incomplete fee descriptions are frequently flagged by ASIC.
🚫 Avoid
"Free" — unless truly zero cost
If there are any indirect costs, transaction costs, or other charges, you cannot claim the product is "free." Even small costs break this claim.
🚫 Avoid
"Low fees" — without basis
You need to be able to prove this claim. Compared to what? On what basis? It's usually safer to just state the actual fee without a qualitative judgement.
🚫 Avoid
"No hidden fees" — if other costs exist
If there are withdrawal fees, buy/sell spreads, or indirect costs, you cannot say there are no hidden fees. Clearly disclose all costs.
⚡ Must
Always add "Other costs apply"
Whenever you mention a specific fee, you must also state whether other costs apply. Minimum: "Other costs apply. For more information please read the PDS."
Fee disclosure — rewrite example
🚫 Wrong
"No hidden costs or fees — just $5.00 a month!"
If there's a $1 withdrawal fee or any spread, this is a breach.
✅ Correct
"Simplified fees — $5.00 per month, with a $1.00 fee each time you withdraw.*

*For further details on fees and costs please see the Product Disclosure Statement."
Hard rules
Prohibited Language
Certain words and terms are legally prohibited under Cache's AFSL. Do not use these under any circumstances.
🚫
The following terms cannot be used at all — ever:
"Stockbroker" · "Sharebroker" · "Insurance broker" · "General insurance broker" · "Life insurance broker" · "Financial counsellor" · "Financial counselling"

These activities are not authorised under Cache's AFSL.
🚫
Also prohibited — in specific circumstances:
You cannot use "independent", "impartial", or "unbiased" if you or your employees may receive a commission, bonus, or any other benefit that could influence them. If your firm receives any form of remuneration from product providers, these terms are off limits.
Tricky area
Comparisons
You can compare products, but only when the comparison is genuinely valid and not misleading. Comparing apples to oranges is one of ASIC's top enforcement areas.
Comparisons — what works vs what doesn't
🚫 Don't compare these
• An equity fund's returns vs. a bank term deposit

• A high-growth equity fund vs. a competitor's defensive/cash fund

• A US tech stock portfolio vs. a single Australian index ETF

• Your fund vs. an unidentified benchmark
✅ Safe to compare these
• Two "balanced" funds with very similar asset allocations

• Two options that both track similar indices

• Your fund vs. its own stated benchmark (disclosed in PDS)
⚡ If you do compare
Clearly disclose all differences
Where there are differences in investment objectives, fees, types of investments, or markets — these must be prominently disclosed. Also ensure that the basis for the comparison will remain reasonably consistent going forward, and if comparing over a short period, clearly state the limitations.
High risk area
Influencers & Third Party Promoters
Using influencers or paid promoters to market your fund? They are almost always unlicensed, which means strict rules apply to what they can and cannot say or do.
🚫
Unlicensed third parties CANNOT:
• Provide any general financial advice (no "this is a fantastic investment", "great way to build wealth")
• Recommend or suggest someone should invest
• Provide a unique link/code that triggers a benefit (this constitutes "dealing by arranging" — a licensed activity)
✅ Allowed
Purely factual content
"This is a platform where you can invest in a managed fund." "Users can access up to X investment options." "Download the app here." These are independently verifiable facts, not recommendations.
🚫 Not Allowed
Presenting facts as recommendations
Even factual content can constitute advice if presented in a way that conveys a recommendation to invest — especially when the promoter is being paid. ASIC will assess the overall impression.
🚫 Not Allowed
Writing their own script
Never allow a third party to write or deliver promotional content as if it were their own unprompted opinion. Cache must review and approve all scripts.
⚡ Must
Mandatory disclaimer in their post
Every third-party post must include a compliant disclaimer (see template in Disclaimer Templates section). It must state: it's a paid ad, they're not giving financial advice, and readers should read the PDS/TMD.
Third party disclaimer template
✅ Required disclaimer for influencer/third party posts
"This information is a paid advertisement and has been prepared by [Name of promoter] who is an authorised representative of Cache Investment Management Ltd (ACN 624 306 430 AFSL 514 360) (Cache). All information is general information only and does not take into account your personal circumstances. Before making a financial decision, you should read the relevant PDS and TMD to consider whether the product is right for you. All investments carry risk."
High risk area
Bonuses, Referrals & Incentives
Referral and bonus programs can quickly cross into territory that requires an AFSL. Understand the line before launching any incentive scheme.
🚫
"Dealing by arranging" is a licensed activity. If a third party provides a unique link, code, or voucher that a user must use to receive a special benefit — and both the user AND the promoter receive something — this constitutes "dealing by arranging" under the law. ASIC has explicitly called this out.
🚫 Not Allowed
Unique referral links with dual benefit
A third party provides their followers a unique link. The user gets a benefit (e.g. waived fee, bonus units). The promoter also gets a benefit (commission per sign-up). This is "dealing by arranging" — prohibited without an AFSL.
🚫 Not Allowed
Unique promo codes from third parties
Same issue as unique links — if a third party distributes a code that gives users a unique benefit (and they receive a cut), it's "dealing by arranging."
✅ Allowed
General promotions available to all users
If you run a promotion available to ALL users, and a third party simply advertises that the promotion exists — with no unique code, no unique link, no special benefit added by the promoter — this is fine. They're not adding unique value.
✅ Allowed
Mere referral exception
A person CAN refer investors to you without an AFSL IF they: (1) only say you provide a financial service, (2) explain how to contact you, and (3) disclose any benefit they receive for the referral. They must not provide any additional incentive for signing up.
Greenwashing risk
ESG & Sustainability Claims
ESG is an active enforcement area for ASIC. If your product makes any environmental, ethical, or sustainability claims, the underlying investment strategy must support those claims precisely.
🌿
Greenwashing is ASIC's priority enforcement area. Misrepresenting your product's environmental or ethical credentials — even vaguely — is a breach. ASIC will compare your marketing claims to your PDS methodology.
⚡ Must
Your ESG methodology must be in the PDS
All positive and negative screens, thresholds, and ESG commitments must be described with sufficient precision in the PDS. Marketing must align with — not go beyond — this methodology.
🚫 Don't
Make claims your fund doesn't support
"Does not invest in fossil fuel companies" — when your PDS says you won't invest more than 10% in that sector. Your marketing must match the PDS exactly.
🚫 Don't
Criticise industries you invest in
Posting on LinkedIn criticising gambling companies when your fund has no negative screens on gambling companies = greenwashing. Your public communications must align with your actual investment approach.
🚫 Don't
Use vague ESG terms without explanation
"Ethical", "sustainable", "socially responsible" — these have no precise legal meaning and vary widely. If you use them, explain exactly what they mean in the context of your specific product and point to your PDS methodology.
ESG — prohibited vs compliant language
🚫 Prohibited
"Invest with us and solve the world's problems."

"Be a part of the solution."

"Save future generations."

"Our fund doesn't invest in fossil fuels." (when your PDS says <10% threshold)
✅ Compliant
"For further information on our investment approach and why we consider our product to be ethical, refer to the ESG methodology contained in our PDS."

"Our fund applies the following screens: [list exactly as in PDS]."
Customer content
Testimonials & Customer Reviews
Customer reviews can be powerful — but they carry compliance risk. You are responsible for everything you re-share.
⚠️
When you re-share a review, you are endorsing it as an accurate representation of your fund. If the review is misleading — even if the customer wrote it genuinely — you may be liable. Reviews cannot be edited, so if they don't meet the standard, don't re-share them.
⚡ Must
Reviews must be authentic
ACCC rules: a real customer must have written it, unaltered. You cannot fabricate, edit, or paraphrase customer reviews.
🚫 Don't re-share
Reviews that imply safety or guaranteed returns
"My money is so safe and secure in this fund." / "My money is growing really fast." — These are misleading statements about financial outcomes and cannot be re-shared.
🚫 Don't re-share
Reviews that recommend the product to others
"I love this product and I know you will too." — This constitutes personal advice to readers. Cannot be re-shared.
✅ Safe to re-share
Experience and feature-based reviews
"I found the app really easy to use and the staff were helpful." / "Perfect for first-time investors." / "I love the range of ETFs available." — These describe the product experience, not financial outcomes.

📌 How to display disclaimers
Visibility requirements
✅ Include disclaimer on the same page as the content — never require readers to navigate elsewhere to see it.
✅ Disclaimer can be smaller text but must be clearly legible.
✅ PDS and TMD must be hyperlinked wherever mentioned.
🚫 Do not hide in footnotes, another document, or behind a "click here for disclaimer" link.
🚫 Do not make the disclaimer so long that its length itself deters reading.

Onboarding flow
Required Documents at Onboarding
Before a customer can complete an application, they must see, access, and agree to specific documents.
⚡ Must — before application finalised
Customer must see and agree to all three
1. Product Disclosure Statement (PDS)
2. Financial Services Guide (FSG)
3. Mobile Application Terms & Conditions

The customer must also have access to the Privacy Policy (does not require agreement, just access).
⚡ Must — after account opened (email)
Send copies by email
When a customer opens an account, send an email with a copy of (or link to): PDS, TMD, and FSG. If sending a link, the document at that URL must remain accessible for at least 7 years and must not change.

Onboarding flow
Knock-Out Questions
Before a customer can invest, they must answer a set of suitability questions. This is a DDO requirement. The flow depends on their answers.
🚫
You cannot allow a customer to invest without completing this flow. These questions determine whether the customer is in the target market. Skipping them is a breach of your DDO obligations.
⚡ First knock-out questions (growth products)
Ask all three — Yes/No answers required
1. Are you looking for capital growth?
2. Do you have a long investment timeframe?
3. Are you willing to risk capital loss?

If YES to all: Show TMD, get acknowledgement they are in the target market → allow to proceed.
If NO to any: Do not allow to invest (unless a cash/capital preservation option exists — then show second knock-out questions).
⚡ Second knock-out questions (capital preservation products)
Only shown if customer answered No above AND a cash option exists
1. Are you looking to preserve capital?
2. Is your investment timeframe not tied to a minimum period?
3. Are you not willing to risk capital loss?

If YES to all: Show cash option TMD, get acknowledgement → allow to invest in cash option only.
If NO to any: Customer cannot invest. Do not allow to proceed.
⚡ After knock-out — customer must confirm
Three acknowledgements required before proceeding
1. I have received the TMD [link].
2. I have read and understand the TMD.
3. To the best of my understanding, I am in the target market for the investment option I intend to acquire.
Onboarding flow
KYC, Identity & Tax
Before collecting personal details, customers must be informed about how their information will be used. Tax residency must be collected for FATCA/CRS compliance.
KYC disclaimer — show before requesting personal details Your personal information will be checked against third party records, including a credit reporting agency and the Australian Government's Document Verification Service (DVS), to assess whether your personal information matches those records and to assist verify your identity for the purposes of the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Act 2006. If you disagree with having your personal information shared with third parties or your identity verified we may not be able to open an account for you. By continuing, you agree that you are authorised to provide us these details and you understand that it will be checked against third party records for this purpose.
⚡ Must collect
Email address + mobile number
Both must be verified during onboarding — either by verification code (SMS) or verification link (email).
⚡ Must ask
Tax residency declaration
"In addition to being an Australian resident for tax purposes, are you a resident for tax purposes of any other country?" If yes: collect country name and Tax Identification Number. If no: request Australian TFN (not mandatory to provide — but withholding tax applies if not given). Do NOT give tax advice — say vaguely "not providing your TFN may have tax implications."
⚡ KYC failure email
Send this email if identity verification fails
The email must include the text: "We were unable to verify your identity against various electronic sources." And request two of: Australian Driver's Licence, Medicare Card, Passport, Visa, Citizenship Certificate, Birth Certificate, Change of Name Certificate, or Centrelink Card.
Transaction security
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
2FA is mandatory for all money-movement instructions and direct debit setup. This cannot be skipped.
🔐
2FA is mandatory every time a customer sets up a direct debit AND every time they give an instruction to move money. Send a code to their registered mobile or email. Customer must type the code into the app.
⚡ Must trigger 2FA for
All of these actions require 2FA every time
1. Setting up a direct debit instruction
2. Providing an instruction to invest (including setting up a recurring auto-investment)
3. Providing an instruction to withdraw funds
4. Providing an instruction to switch between investment options
Transaction flow
Transaction Confirmation Screens
Before submitting any transaction, the customer must see a confirmation screen with all relevant details and actively press "Confirm". You cannot submit a transaction to Cache's API unless the customer confirmed it.
⚡ Investment confirmation must show
For applications / investments
1. Date (instruction submission)
2. Investment Option
3. Investment Amount
4. Fees (incl. GST) — show "Nil" if none
5. Total Amount (gross of fees)
⚡ Withdrawal confirmation must show
For withdrawals / redemptions
1. Date (instruction submission)
2. Investment Option
3. Withdrawal Amount
4. Fees (incl. GST) — show "Nil" if none
5. Proceeds (net of fees)
⚡ Switch confirmation must show
For switches between options
1. Date (instruction submission)
2. Switch Out Investment Option
3. Switch Out amount in $
4. Switch In Investment Option
5. Fees (incl. GST) — show "Nil" if none
⚡ Transaction confirmation email
Send after unitisation (not just on submission)
Once a transaction is accepted AND unitised, send a confirmation email showing: RE name, IM name, transaction date, investment option, description, transaction number, amounts, units, unit price, and fees.

Collect bank details at onboarding

2FA every time direct debit is set up
Each time a customer sets up a direct debit instruction (not each payment, just setup), 2FA is required.

Full Direct Debit Request and Service Agreement text must be available to the customer — either displayed in full or via a popup on the same page as the disclaimer. See the UI Requirements document for the full Zepto DDR text.
In-app display
Performance Information Display
Anywhere performance data appears in your app — charts, tables, graphs — specific rules apply.
⚡ Must — next to every performance chart/table
Mandatory performance disclaimer
"These returns are not a projection. Actual returns may differ and can be positive or negative. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns."

This must appear directly adjacent to the performance data — not on a separate page or in a tooltip the user must actively open.
⚡ Must
Past tense with specified timeframes
"We outperformed the market over the 12 months to 30 June 2021" ✅
"We outperform the market" 🚫 — No timeframe, present tense = misleading.
⚡ Must
All performance content reviewed by Cache
Any in-app performance display (charts, historical returns, index comparisons) must be reviewed by Cache under RG 53 before going live. Submit for review via the Cache Jira board.
⚡ Must
Index vs fund — make the difference clear
If you show index performance anywhere, you must make it unambiguous that this is the index's performance, not the fund's. A specific disclaimer (drafted case-by-case with Cache) is required.
