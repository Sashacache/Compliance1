The Golden Rules
Before writing a single word of marketing content, understand these non-negotiable principles. Everything else in this guide flows from them.
🚫
Never Do
  • Promise or imply returns
  • Give personal financial advice
  • Use prohibited terms
  • Omit risks when mentioning benefits
  • Use unverified statistics
  • Misrepresent ESG credentials
  • Allow unlicensed 3rd parties to give advice
  • Give tax advice
⚠️
Requires Care
  • General advice (needs documented rationale)
  • Past performance data
  • Projections & forecasts
  • Comparisons with other products
  • Testimonials from customers
  • ESG / sustainability claims
  • Fee comparisons
  • Cross-selling to existing investors
✅
Generally Safe
  • Verified factual statements (with source)
  • Describing how the product works
  • Explaining the investment process
  • Factual product features
  • Directing people to the PDS/TMD
  • Providing compliant disclaimers
⚡
The overarching rule — Corporations Act s1041H You must not engage in conduct that is misleading or deceptive — or likely to mislead or deceive — in relation to a financial product. Applies to wholesale AND retail investors. No minimum threshold. Even a small inaccuracy counts. Intent is irrelevant — you can breach the law accidentally. ASIC assesses the total impression including pictures, animations, tone and language.
📋
All promotional material must be submitted to Cache before use. Submit via the Cache Marketing Jira Board. Contact sasha.inquimbert@cacheinvest.com.au for access. Attach as a Word document. Do not use Google Docs links or PDFs that can't be marked up.
⚡ Must
You must be licensed
Anyone providing financial product advice must be an authorised representative of Cache's AFSL. Both the corporate entity AND the individual must be registered with ASIC.
⚡ Must
RG146 training required
Anyone speaking to retail clients, producing promotional materials, or involved in distribution must be RG146 compliant. Cache requires 20 hours of CPD per year. Submit your training register to Cache annually.
⚡ Must
TMD must exist before marketing
A valid Target Market Determination must be in place before promoting a retail fund. All marketing must align with — not contradict — the TMD. Breach of s994D is a civil penalty provision.
⚡ Must
Total impression test
ASIC assesses whether content is misleading based on the overall impression — including pictures, animations, tone and language. A technically accurate statement can still be misleading in context.
Content types
Advice vs Factual Information
Every piece of content falls into one of three categories. Knowing which one determines what rules apply to you.
✅
Preferred approach — lowest regulatory risk. Factual information is objectively verifiable. The truth or accuracy cannot reasonably be questioned. Always include a source.
✅ Factual Information Examples
✅ Safe to say
"The ASX200 rose 17% in 2021."
Verifiable fact. Include source (e.g. ASX website).
✅ Safe to say
"Our Fund invests primarily in Australian and international ETFs."
Factual product description. Must cross-check against PDS.
Superlative rewriting — how to make claims safer
🚫 Hard to verify
"We are the fastest growing company in this space."

"We allow you to invest in the most popular industry."
Superlatives require absolute proof. Almost impossible to substantiate.
✅ Do this instead
"We are a fast-growing company in this space."

"We allow you to invest in a popular industry."
Still needs factual basis and source, but far more defensible.
⚡ Must
Always include a source
Every factual claim needs a source — hyperlink in the text body or under a "Sources" heading. Cache will ask for it if missing.
⚠️
Use with care. Requires documented rationale. General advice involves a qualitative judgement or recommendation about a financial product. Cache may ask you to remove advice elements.
⚠️ General Advice — Don't say this without proper documentation
🚫 Don't say without briefing note
"Now may be a good time to invest in shares."
Qualitative judgement. Requires a documented briefing note explaining why now specifically is better than other times.
✅ Safer alternative
"Share markets have shown resilience over the past 12 months, with the ASX200 returning X% to [date]. [Source]"
Factual and verifiable — no qualitative judgement about what investors should do.
Another general advice rewrite
🚫 General advice (requires documented reasoning)
"Invest now in one of Australia's leading ethical funds!"
This is a recommendation. Requires documented reasoning and a general advice disclaimer.
✅ Do this instead
"Our fund applies an ESG methodology as described in our PDS. Learn more about how we invest and whether it aligns with your values."
Factual — points to the PDS without making a recommendation to invest.
⚡ Must
Document your reasoning
If you make a qualitative statement you must genuinely hold that opinion AND provide comprehensive, documented reasons — to a standard a professional financial services provider could reasonably rely on.
🚫
Never provide personal financial advice. As an Authorised Representative of Cache's AFSL, you are NOT authorised to provide personal financial advice. Only registered financial planners can do this — and only after learning about someone's personal circumstances.
🚫 Personal Advice — Always Prohibited vs What To Say Instead
🚫 Never say
"Based on your salary of $80,000 and current investments, you should invest $20,000 per year in this ETF."

"Our product would be great for you."

"You will love our product."

"Our product was designed for people just like you!"
✅ Do this instead
"This product may be appropriate for investors seeking [description from TMD]. Please read the TMD to see if you are in the target market, and consider seeking personal financial advice before investing."

"Our product is designed for [target market description]. Read the PDS to see how it works."
Refer to the TMD description. Never personalise to an individual's circumstances.
High risk area
Future Performance & Returns
One of the most commonly breached areas. Any statement that implies a positive future return is almost always prohibited — even vague ones.
🚫
Cache will NOT approve vague or non-specific future return statements. Representations about future matters must be based on reasonable grounds. If they aren't, the law treats them as misleading — automatically.
Future performance — every prohibited phrase with a safer alternative
🚫 Prohibited
"Get rich with us."

"Earn up to 12% p.a."

"Start seeing your money grow."

"Make money on your money."

"Save with us."

"Earn high returns with low risk."

"To the moon baby."

"Get fantastic returns on your investment through our Fund."
✅ Do this instead
"You can start investing now via our App."

"The Balanced Option returned X% net of fees for the year to 30 June 2024.* [past performance disclaimer]"

"You can begin your investing journey with us."

"Build an investment portfolio through our platform."

"Invest alongside others in a managed fund structure."

"This product is designed for investors seeking higher returns and who are willing to accept higher risk." (only if consistent with the TMD)
Focus on what the product IS and HOW it works — not what it will deliver.
Projections & Forecasts — Only Permitted When All 4 Conditions Are Met
⚡ Condition 1
Index-tracking products only
Only permitted for products tracking a specific index (e.g. ETF tracking S&P/ASX 200). Not for actively managed funds.
⚡ Condition 2
Minimum 10 years of historical data
You must have at least 10 years of index data to support the return and volatility assumptions.
⚡ Condition 3
Full methodology provided to Cache
Detailed enough that Cache can precisely replicate the projection. Assumptions must be explicitly stated.
⚡ Condition 4
Update every 6 months
All forecasts must be reviewed and updated every 6 months to remain current and not misleading.
⚡ Must include alongside any projection
Mandatory statements
1. Specific methodology and data used.
2. All assumptions underpinning the forecast.
3. Statement that forecasted returns may not arise and the client's balance may fall.
4. Reference to the Product Disclosure Statement.
Compliant projection example (from Cache guide)
✅ How a compliant projection disclaimer looks
"Calculated at $200 contribution monthly at 7% p.a. historical return rate for 10 years, compounded monthly.

The backtested annualised return for the Balanced Investment Option over the past 10 years to 31st December 2021 is 7%. The backtest is net of expected fees and costs and is based on the Investment Option target asset allocation against the respective benchmarks, namely, S&P/ASX 200 Total Returns Index. The total returns nature of the index means that any dividends received are assumed to be re-invested back into the index but does not include any adjustments for withholding tax. Past performance is not an indicator of future performance."
Requires care
Past Performance & Returns
Past performance can be used, but strict rules govern calculation, presentation, and mandatory disclaimers.
Core rewrite — the most common mistake
🚫 Wrong
"The Fund has provided returns of over 20% to its investors over the last 5 years."
No disclaimer, no specification of which option, no methodology, no net-of-fees statement.
✅ Correct
"The Balanced Investment Option returned 13.4% for the financial year to 30 June 2021.*

*Returns are net of investment fees and taxes. These returns are not a projection. Actual returns may differ, and can be positive or negative. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns."
⚡ Must
Calculate net of fees and taxes
You cannot show a raw change in unit price if fees are charged separately. Returns must be net of investment fees and taxes. Where some fees can't be deducted, clearly state this.
⚡ Must
Display the mandatory disclaimer
Directly under or next to any performance data: "These returns are not a projection. Actual returns may differ and can be positive or negative. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns."
Cherry-picking short periods — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
Publish only the 3-month return of 8% (when the full-year return was 2%).

Or: "January was our best month ever — up 4%!" with no other context.
Cherry-picking a short good period is misleading even if the number is accurate.
✅ Do this instead
"The Growth Option returned 4% for January 2024. Over the 12 months to 31 January 2024, the option returned 2.1%.* [past performance disclaimer]"
Show the short period alongside the full-year or inception-to-date figure for context.
Benchmark omission — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
"Our fund returned 5% last year." (When the benchmark returned 15% over the same period.)
✅ Do this instead
"Our fund returned 5% over the year to 30 June 2024, compared to the benchmark (S&P/ASX 200) return of 15% over the same period.* [disclaimer]"
Always show benchmark alongside fund performance if the fund tracks or targets one.
Multiple investment options — don't aggregate
🚫 Don't
"Our fund returned an average of 9% across all options last year."
✅ Do this instead
"Growth Option: 12% | Balanced Option: 8% | Conservative Option: 4% — all for the year to 30 June 2024, net of fees.* [disclaimer]"
Show each option separately. Investors need to know what their specific option returned.
Hypothetical statistics — using external index data
🚫 Don't
"The ASX top 200 returned 10% p.a. over the past 10 years — invest with us to access this growth." (When your fund has historically returned 8%.)
✅ Do this instead
"The ASX 200 returned 10% p.a. over the past 10 years [source]. Our Growth Option, which tracks the ASX 200, returned 11% p.a. over the same period net of fees.* [disclaimer]"
You can only use external index data alongside your own performance if your fund outperformed it over the same period.
🚫 Never
Annualise periods under 12 months
Any performance period less than a year must NOT be annualised. Clearly state the exact period it covers (e.g. "3 months to 31 March 2024").

✅ Instead say: "For the 3 months to 31 March 2024, the option returned 2.1%." — never: "annualised return of 8.4%."
Balance required
Risks & Benefits
Every time you mention a benefit — even indirectly — you must also mention the associated risk. This applies even to educational content not specific to your product.
⚠️
The rule: Whenever your content implies or states a benefit (ease of investing, low fees, compound interest, diversification), you must include a corresponding risk statement.
Benefit without risk — rewrite
🚫 Don't — benefit with no risk
"Our App provides an easy, all-in-one destination where you can invest in some of Australia's most popular ETFs. Signing-up is easy — start your investment journey today!"
No risk mention = automatic problem.
✅ Do this instead
"Our App provides an easy, all-in-one destination where you can invest in some of Australia's most popular ETFs. Signing-up is easy — start your investment journey today!**

**There are risks to investing in ETFs. You may lose money invested. Please read the Product Disclosure Statement, available here."
Educational content also needs risk disclosure
🚫 Don't
"A series of articles about the benefits of investing in shares — why shares have historically outperformed cash, the power of dividends, compound growth." (No mention of risk anywhere.)
✅ Do this instead
Include a section or callout: "Investing in shares carries risk. Share prices can fall as well as rise and you may receive less than you invested. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns. Consider seeking financial advice."
⚡ Minimum risk disclaimer for social/short-form
Use this as a minimum
"All investments carry risk. You may lose your money invested. For more information please read the Product Disclosure Statement."

Longer ads that detail specific benefits may require a more detailed risk description.
Common mistake area
Fees & Costs
Fee disclosures must give a realistic impression. Vague or incomplete fee descriptions are frequently flagged by ASIC.
"Free" claim — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
"Invest for free!" or "No fees — invest today!"
If there are any indirect costs, transaction costs, or charges, you cannot claim it's free.
✅ Do this instead
"No management fee for the first 3 months.* *Other costs apply. See PDS for full fee details."
Only claim "free" for what is genuinely free — and always disclose what else applies.
"Low fees" claim — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
"Invest with low fees." (No further explanation or qualification.)
Compared to what? On what basis? Very difficult to substantiate without context.
✅ Do this instead
"Management fee: 0.65% p.a. Other costs may apply. See PDS for full fee and cost details."
Simply state the actual fee. Let investors make their own comparison.
"No hidden fees" claim — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
"No hidden costs or fees — just $5.00 a month!"
If there's a $1 withdrawal fee, buy/sell spread, or any indirect cost — this is a breach.
✅ Do this instead
"Simplified fees — $5.00 per month, with a $1.00 fee each time you withdraw.* *For further details on all fees and costs please see the Product Disclosure Statement."
⚡ Must
Always disclose "Other costs apply"
Whenever you mention a specific fee, you must state whether other costs apply. Minimum: "Other costs apply. For more information please read the PDS."
Hard rules
Prohibited Language
Certain words are legally prohibited under Cache's AFSL. Do not use these under any circumstances.
🚫
These terms cannot be used — ever:
"Stockbroker" · "Sharebroker" · "Insurance broker" · "General insurance broker" · "Life insurance broker" · "Financial counsellor" · "Financial counselling"

These activities are not authorised under Cache's AFSL.
Prohibited terms — rewrite examples
🚫 Don't use
"Our financial counsellors are ready to help."

"Speak to one of our stockbrokers today."

"Our independent advisers provide unbiased advice."
✅ Do this instead
"Our client services team can answer questions about how the platform works."

"Contact our team to learn more about the fund."

"Our advisers can provide general information about the fund." (Remove "independent/unbiased" if any commissions apply.)
🚫
Also prohibited in specific circumstances: "Independent", "impartial", or "unbiased" — if you or your employees may receive a commission, bonus, or any other benefit that could influence them. These terms are off limits if any remuneration flows from product providers.
Tricky area
Comparisons
You can compare products — but only when the comparison is genuinely valid. Comparing apples to oranges is a top ASIC enforcement area.
Invalid comparisons — don't vs do
🚫 Don't compare these
An equity fund's returns vs. a bank term deposit.

A high-growth equity fund vs. a competitor's defensive/cash fund.

A US tech stock portfolio vs. a single Australian index ETF.

Your fund vs. an unidentified cash benchmark.
ASIC has specifically called out these types of misleading comparisons in their 2022 managed funds review.
✅ Safe to compare
Two "balanced" funds with very similar asset allocations.

Two investment options that both track similar indices.

Your fund vs. its own stated benchmark (disclosed in the PDS).

Different fee structures within your own product offering.
If there are material differences, clearly disclose them — including investment objectives, fees, and markets covered.
⚡ If you compare, you must
Clearly disclose all differences
Where there are differences in investment objectives, fees, types of investments, or markets — these must be prominently disclosed. Ensure the basis for comparison will remain reasonably consistent going forward. If comparing over a short period, clearly state the limitations.
High risk area
Influencers & Third Party Promoters
Influencers and paid promoters are almost always unlicensed. Strict rules govern what they can say and do.
🚫
Unlicensed third parties CANNOT:
• Provide any general financial advice
• Recommend or suggest someone should invest
• Provide a unique link/code that triggers a benefit (this is "dealing by arranging" — a licensed activity)
• Write or deliver their own script as if it were their unprompted opinion
What influencers can and cannot say
🚫 Not allowed (even if "just their opinion")
"This product is a fantastic investment — I put my own money in it."

"Using this fund is a great way to supercharge your portfolio."

"I think now's the perfect time to invest — get in now."
Even presenting facts in a way that conveys a recommendation to invest can constitute advice — especially if the influencer is paid.
✅ Allowed — purely factual content
"This is a platform where you can invest in a managed fund."

"Users can access up to X investment options."

"The app is available on iOS and Android — download it here."

"[Fund name] is running a webinar next week — register at [link]."
Independently verifiable facts only. Educational and balanced perspective — not espousing virtues.
Required disclaimer — every third party post must include this
✅ Mandatory disclaimer for influencer/third party posts
"This information is a paid advertisement and has been prepared by [Name of promoter] who is an authorised representative of Cache Investment Management Ltd (ACN 624 306 430 AFSL 514 360) (Cache). All information is general information only and does not take into account your personal circumstances. Before making a financial decision, you should read the relevant PDS and TMD to consider whether the product is right for you. All investments carry risk."
Mere Referral Exception — When a Third Party CAN Refer Without an AFSL
✅ Allowed under exception
What a referrer can do
1. Tell the potential customer you are able to provide a financial service.
2. Explain how they can contact you.
3. Disclose any benefit (commission) they receive for the referral.

That's it — nothing more.
🚫 Exception does not apply if
These conditions break the exception
The referrer also provides a unique link, code, or special incentive for investing. If they receive a benefit per sign-up AND the user gets a unique benefit, this is "dealing by arranging" — not mere referral.
High risk area
Bonuses, Referrals & Incentives
Referral and bonus programs can quickly cross into territory requiring an AFSL. Understand the line before launching any incentive scheme.
🚫
"Dealing by arranging" is a licensed activity. If a third party provides a unique link, code, or voucher that triggers a special benefit — and both the user AND the promoter receive something — this is "dealing by arranging." ASIC has explicitly identified this.
Bonus structures — what's allowed vs what's not
🚫 Not allowed
A third party shares a unique referral link on social media. Users who click get a waived fee or bonus units. The influencer earns a commission per sign-up.

A third party distributes a promo code. Users enter it in the app to get a unique benefit. The promoter also receives a payment.
✅ Allowed
You run a promotion available to ALL users (e.g. "no fee for first 3 months"). A third party simply posts that this promotion exists — using no unique link or code. The promoter adds no unique value beyond publicising the pre-existing offer.

A referrer tells their audience that your fund exists and how to contact you, and discloses they receive a fee for referrals. No unique link or code. No special benefit per sign-up.
Greenwashing risk
ESG & Sustainability Claims
ESG is an active ASIC enforcement area. Any environmental, ethical, or sustainability claim must be precisely supported by your actual investment strategy.
🌿
Greenwashing is ASIC's current enforcement priority. Misrepresenting environmental or ethical credentials — even vaguely — is a breach. ASIC compares your marketing claims directly to your PDS methodology.
ESG claims — prohibited vs compliant
🚫 Prohibited
"Invest with us and solve the world's problems."

"Be a part of the solution."

"Save future generations."

"Our fund doesn't invest in fossil fuels." (when your PDS says the fund won't invest in more than 10% in that sector)

Posting a LinkedIn article criticising gambling when your fund has no gambling negative screen.
✅ Do this instead
"Our fund applies ESG screens as described in our PDS. These include [list exactly what is in your PDS]."

"Our fund will not invest in companies that derive more than 10% of revenue from thermal coal. See our PDS for our full ESG methodology."

"For further information on our investment approach and why we consider our product to be ethical, refer to the ESG methodology in our PDS."
Your public statements must precisely match your PDS commitments — no more, no less.
Vague ESG terms — how to handle them
🚫 Don't use vague terms alone
"Our fund is ethical."

"We invest sustainably."

"Socially responsible investing."
"Ethical", "sustainable", "socially responsible" have no precise legal meaning and vary widely between products.
✅ Do this instead
"Our fund applies ethical screens — we exclude companies deriving more than X% revenue from [sectors]. For our full methodology, see Section X of our PDS."

"We apply an ESG integration approach. Refer to our PDS for what 'sustainable' means in the context of our fund."
Always define what the term means for YOUR specific product and link to the PDS for detail.
⚡ Must
ESG methodology must be in the PDS first
All screens, thresholds, and ESG commitments must be described with sufficient precision in the PDS before you can make any ESG claims in marketing. Marketing must align with — not go beyond — what the PDS commits to.
Customer content
Testimonials & Customer Reviews
You are responsible for everything you re-share. If the review is misleading, you may be liable — even if the customer wrote it genuinely.
⚠️
When you re-share a review, you are endorsing it as accurate. Reviews cannot be edited. If a review doesn't meet the standard, don't re-share it.
Reviews — what you can and cannot re-share
🚫 Don't re-share these
"My money is so safe and secure in this fund." ❌ (Misleading financial outcome claim)

"My money is growing really fast with this fund." ❌ (Implies performance guarantee)

"I love this investment product and I know you will too." ❌ (Personal advice to readers)
✅ Safe to re-share
"I found the app really easy to use and the staff were so helpful." ✅

"Perfect for first-time investors." ✅

"I love that the fund offers a wide range of ETFs and ESG investments." ✅
Safe reviews describe the product experience and features — not financial outcomes or returns.
⚡ Must
Reviews must be authentic and unaltered
ACCC rules: a real customer must have written it, and it must not be altered in any way. You cannot fabricate, edit, or paraphrase customer reviews.
DDO obligation
Cross-Selling
Cross-selling is when you promote other products to existing investors. It carries DDO obligations and privacy risks.
⚠️
The Firstmac case: Firstmac was found in breach of DDO obligations when it used personal details of fixed term depositors to market higher-risk products to them. Cross-selling must only occur between products with matching TMD attributes.
Cross-selling — acceptable vs prohibited
🚫 Don't
Market a high-growth equity fund to investors who originally signed up for a conservative fixed-income fund. The TMD risk profiles are incompatible.
✅ Acceptable
Market a second balanced growth fund to investors already in a balanced growth fund — where both products share the same TMD attributes (risk/return, liquidity, investment timeframe, concentration, objectives).
⚡ Must
Cross-sell only to matching TMD profiles
The product you cross-sell must have the same TMD attributes as the product the investor currently holds: same risk/return, liquidity, investment objective, concentration, and investment timeframe.
⚡ Must
Check the Privacy Framework
Using investor personal details for cross-selling requires compliance with your privacy obligations. Check the Cache Privacy Framework before using existing investor data for any new product marketing.
Hard prohibition
Tax Advice
Cache is not registered to give tax advice. Neither are you as an Authorised Representative. Any tax-related content requires careful handling.
🚫
Never include advice about the tax implications of an investment. This includes statements about tax deductibility, CGT treatment, or how the fund will affect someone's tax position.
Tax statements — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
"Investing in our fund is tax-efficient."

"Your distributions may be tax-free under a managed fund structure."

"This ETF is a great way to minimise your CGT liability."
✅ Do this instead
"This investment may produce tax consequences. You may wish to seek your own professional tax advice about the applicable tax consequences that may apply to this investment."

"You can find out more about tax and how it may relate to investments by visiting the ATO website."

📌 How to display disclaimers
Visibility requirements
✅ Include on the same page as the content — never require readers to navigate elsewhere.
✅ Can be smaller text but must be clearly legible and readable.
✅ PDS and TMD must be hyperlinked wherever mentioned.
🚫 Never hide in footnotes, another document, or behind a "click here for disclaimer" link.
🚫 Never make it so long that its length itself deters reading.

ASIC found in 2022 managed funds review: warnings not sufficiently clear or prominent; risks not present or not prominent enough; disclaimers inconsistent with fund strategy.

Onboarding — DDO requirement
Knock-Out Questions
Before a customer can invest, they must answer suitability questions. The flow depends on their answers. Skipping this is a breach of DDO obligations.
🚫
You cannot allow a customer to invest without completing this flow. These questions determine whether the customer is in the target market. This is a civil penalty provision.
First Knock-Out Questions — Growth / Capital Risk Products
⚡ Ask all three — Yes/No answers
First set of questions
1. Are you looking for capital growth? (Yes/No)
2. Do you have a long investment timeframe? (Yes/No)
3. Are you willing to risk capital loss? (Yes/No)
✅ If YES to all three
Show this message then get acknowledgement
"Before making an application to invest please take note that the Fund may have other investment options; and please ensure that before acquiring an investment option in the Fund you read the TMD [link] to ensure the investment option you intend to acquire aligns with your objectives."

Customer must then confirm:
1. I have received the TMD [link]
2. I have read and understand the TMD
3. To the best of my understanding I am in the target market
🚫 If NO to any question
Do not allow to invest (unless cash option exists)
If the customer answers NO to any first knock-out question, they cannot invest in the growth product.

If a cash or capital preservation option is available, show the second knock-out questions below. Otherwise, hard stop — the customer cannot proceed.
Second Knock-Out Questions — Capital Preservation Products (only if applicable)
⚡ Only shown if cash/preservation option exists
Second set of questions
1. Are you looking to preserve capital? (Yes/No)
2. Is your investment timeframe not tied to a minimum period? (Yes/No)
3. Are you not willing to risk capital loss? (Yes/No)
✅ If YES to all three
Show capital preservation TMD, get acknowledgement
Show the same message and get the same three confirmations (received TMD, read and understood, in target market) — but for the cash/preservation investment option only.
🚫 If NO to any second question
Hard stop — cannot invest
The customer cannot invest in any option. Do not allow to proceed. Show a message explaining they are unable to invest at this time and suggest they seek financial advice.

Onboarding
Tax Residency Declaration
Tax residency must be collected for FATCA/CRS compliance. Do not give tax advice — keep any explanatory text vague.
⚡ Must ask during onboarding
Required question
"In addition to being an Australian resident for tax purposes, are you a resident for tax purposes of any other country?" (Yes/No)

If Yes: Collect the name of the other country (or countries) AND the relevant Tax Identification Number for each country (equivalent to a TFN).

If No: Collect the Australian Tax File Number. Note: requesting the TFN is required, but providing it is not mandatory. If the customer does not provide it, withholding tax may be applied.
TFN explanation — what you can say
🚫 Don't say
"If you don't provide your TFN, you will pay more tax."

"Your TFN is required to reduce your tax withholding rate."
This constitutes tax advice — prohibited.
✅ Do say (vague only)
"Not providing your TFN may have tax implications."
This is intentionally vague. Do not elaborate further.
Transaction security
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
2FA is mandatory for all money-movement instructions and direct debit setup. This cannot be skipped under any circumstances.
🔐
2FA is mandatory every time a customer gives a money-movement instruction or sets up a direct debit. Send a code to their registered mobile or email. The customer must type the code into the app.
⚡ Must trigger 2FA for all of these
Every one of these actions requires 2FA every time
1. Setting up a direct debit instruction
2. Providing an instruction to invest (including recurring/auto-investment setup)
3. Providing an instruction to withdraw funds
4. Providing an instruction to switch between investment options

In-app display
Performance Information Display
Anywhere performance data appears in your app — charts, tables, graphs — specific rules apply. All performance content must be reviewed by Cache before going live (RG 53).
⚡ Must — adjacent to every performance chart or table
Mandatory performance disclaimer
"These returns are not a projection. Actual returns may differ and can be positive or negative. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future returns."

This must appear directly adjacent to the performance data — not on a separate page or in a tooltip the user must actively open.
Performance tense and timeframe — don't vs do
🚫 Don't
"We outperform the market."

"The fund is currently beating its benchmark."
Present tense without a timeframe is misleading.
✅ Do this instead
"We outperformed the market over the 12 months to 30 June 2021."

"The fund returned X% over the 3 years to 31 December 2023, compared to the benchmark return of Y% over the same period."
Always past tense + specific period + benchmark (if applicable).
⚡ Must
Index vs fund — make the difference explicit
If you show index performance anywhere alongside fund performance, you must make it unambiguous which is which. A specific disclaimer (drafted case-by-case with Cache) is required. Do not allow users to conflate index returns with fund returns.
⚡ Must
Submit all performance displays to Cache for review
Any in-app performance display (charts, historical returns tables, index comparisons) must be reviewed by Cache under RG 53 before going live. Submit via the Jira board.
